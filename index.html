H2O JUST ADD WATER:
TV REVIEW

What h2o is about?

The one TV show I've always watched since childhood is "H2O: Just Add Water," it is an Australian series that depicts the story of 3 teenage girls becoming mermaids. Sure, mermaids might be considered for kids, but hey, I’m young at heart. What makes me love this show,
is the genuine feel of the storyline and the way its filmed seemed realistic. Whether as a child or an adult, watching it makes me appreciate the serenity of the ocean.

Why I love h2o just add water?

I adore this show is not just its Australian backdrop, though Australia's beauty adds to it. The main reason is the strong friendship among the girls— Rikki, Cleo, and Emma — is more than becoming mermaids. Their whole story began when they were stranded on "Mako Island," and they had no idea that their lives were about to change forever.
The change brought both joy and challenges. The ocean felt magical, offering a sense of freedom,
but the girls had to be cautious in public, as any contact with water turned them into mermaids in just 10 seconds. This secret had to be guarded from everyone, even family members.
Though the show might seem cringe at first glance, trust me, it's captivating. Once you start, you can't stop. "H2O: Just Add Water" spans three seasons from 2006 to 2009.
Now, in 2024, I eagerly await a reunion. The show's depiction of friendship and the nostalgia it evokes make it a timeless favorite.
